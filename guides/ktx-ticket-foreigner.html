How to Buy KTX Tickets as a Foreigner (2026)

Korea's KTX gets you Seoul → Busan in about 2.5 hours. The hard part isn't the train — it's paying without your foreign card getting rejected. Here's the path that actually works.

TL;DR
  • Book online with just your passport on Trip.com, Klook or KKday — official Korail partners that accept foreign cards.
  • Korail's own site often fails foreign cards at the identity-verification step. Skip it.
  • Visiting several cities? A Korail Pass (foreigner-only) is often cheaper.
  • Avoid Rail Ninja — big hidden markups.

1. The easy way: book with your passport on a partner site

Trip.com, Klook and KKday are official Korail resellers. You enter your name and passport number, pay with a foreign card (or PayPal), and get a QR/e-ticket. They charge face value plus a small booking fee — typically ₩1,000–2,500 per ticket.

Search KTX on Trip.com KTX & Korail Pass on Klook

2. Why Korail's own website rejects your card

Korail's checkout routes foreign cards through Korea's 본인인증 (identity verification) and 3-D Secure, which expects a Korean ID number and a Korean-issued card. The result is a confusing "payment failed" with no clear reason. Use a partner above, or buy in person.

At any station you can always buy a ticket at the counter or machine with your passport and a card or cash — no app, no account.

3. Multiple cities? Get a Korail Pass

The Korail Pass is a foreigner-only ticket for unlimited train travel (KTX included) over a set number of days. If you're doing, say, Seoul → Busan → Gyeongju → Seoul, it usually beats buying singles. It's sold by Korail and by the same partners.

4. Sellers to avoid

Rail Ninja: not an official seller; reports of 80–90% markups over face value and steep, partial refunds. Stick to Trip.com / Klook / KKday or the station.
